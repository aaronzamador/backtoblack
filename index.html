24 ◦ he / him ◦ cat dad

For me, design has always been in the periphery of my vision, but it wasn’t until a summer in quarantine with a second-hand Meggs' History of Graphic Design that I understood how deeply rooted my passion was. After reading D. Norman’s Design of Everyday Things and Emotional Design, and an incredibly intriguing The Meaning of Things, by M. Csikszentmihalyi and E. Halton I felt a new calling I couldn’t deny, or resist.

Luckily, the skills and views I’ve acquired from my past experience apply to UX design. 10 years working directly with customers–users–means their needs and frustrations are always my top priority. My experience working closely with business owners means I understand the importance of good design to increase productivity and profit. I believe in meshing functionality with beautiful design, because you can’t truly have one without the other.

I’m still learning, growing, and soaking in knowledge–for me, the best feeling in the world is reaching the top of a steep learning curve.
