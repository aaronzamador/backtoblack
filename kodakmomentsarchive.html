Kodak Moments concept design and evolution

Check out the finished prototype

Project scope: User research, innovation strategy, data analysis, archetypes, wireframing, usability testing, stakeholder interview, UI design

Duration: 20 weeks
Team size: 10
My role: UX and visual design

“Today’s markets and technologies are constantly changing, and we’re ready to meet those changes head-on.”

— Kodak Moments team

A little background

In 2013, Eastman Kodak Company created a spin-off business–Kodak Alaris. Kodak Moments, a division of Kodak Alaris, is a global leader in consumer photo printing, offering photo products and services to retailers, consumers, and entertainment properties. Their goal is to maintain the Kodak brand’s rich, global heritage, and inject it with a new speed and agility.

The goal

Accelerate the growth of the Kodak Moments platform by identifying a strategic direction for future growth, creating an innovative concept, and delivering a recommended roadmap to implementation with development ready designs.

Working with strategists

Our strategists analyzed competitors and interviewed consumers to identify five potential areas of growth for Kodak Moments.

Competative analysis

Findings show that process of a taking a photo and getting it in print can sometimes span up to seven different competitors.

Consumer archetypes

Our strategists found five consumer archetypes. Their processes vary, but all find a common ground in editing and sharing memories.

Concept selection

Speaking with consumers and Kodak Moments and discussing brand direction helped guide the decision between five concepts.

Concept categories ranged from Colorful White Label partnerships, API to App partnerships, focusing on their current Tech Debt, and Home Decor. Kodak Moments found the Home Decor sector the most appealing, and our Customize My Space concept was born. With the direction decided, strategists turned to the designers to bring the concept to life.

The Concept–Customize My Space

The power of this concept is the ability to move Kodak Moments into a new channel. Consumers see the existing process for framing prints as inefficient and would welcome a solution that makes it easier.

By uploading a photo of the consumer's home, we're able to show you how a frame and photo would look in your space. By creating relationships with partners like Ikea or West Elm, the photo you select will be automatically printed at the correct size based on the frame you choose and shipped to your home.

Key features

  • In store QR code

  • Home decor recommendations

  • Layout and frame recommendations

  • Photo edit recommendations

  • AR or model room viewing

  • Direct purchase from one place

User flow

Kodak Moments partnerships with home decor retailers is critical to this concept. With that in mind, I created a user flow, bringing the consumer from a partner's website into the Kodak Moments takeover and finishing with a purchase. Through presentations with the frog team and Kodak Moments, a general flow was decided on and I transitioned to wireframing.

❮ ❯

Wireframes

Over three weeks, I wireframed the user flow and presented to the frog and Kodak Moments team. With their feedback, several pieces of the flow began to emerge.

In the last of the three weeks, the visual designer and I worked together to iterate designs and integrate Kodak Moments' existing design language.

❮ ❯

Consumer feedback

Pressure testing the concept identified areas of opportunity, but also underwrote the decision for choosing this direction. Homes are highly personal spaces, and all-in-one solutions for decorating that space is rare and rich for development

"I'm a busy guy with three kids and a job, so I can't sit around and edit photos or look at frames for hours. It's not a fun process, but these are important memories."

— Consumer

Key takeaways

  • This experience improved consumer's perception of the home decor retailer.

  • Consumers are interested in complex gallery walls, but are afraid to try and execute them.

  • Choosing frames, uploading photos, and previewing on a wall felt like an all-in-one experience.

  • All recommendations–Frame settings, photo edits, wall layouts–were received positively.

Journey map

I iterated on the journey map throughout the program, focused on creating something not only informational for the current phase of the work, but also inspiring to the Kodak Moments team. Current, MVP, and future emotional states were mapped to concepts and consumer insights, adding screen designs to further illustrate the steps.

Hi-Fi screens

With an MVP approach, I continued to iterate using the Kodak Moments design language. Editing funcationalities, shopping capabilities and the wall viewing experience were improved and retested before final delivery. Below are the final screen designs and the clickable prototype.

Check out the finished prototype

Final thoughts

Collaborating with Kodak Moments and the frog team to bring this concept to life was exciting and educational. Working with a team of strategists that brought so much to the table, and learning to speak in their language was a formative challenge. Leading design reviews with the Kodak Moments team was fun and informative, their team's involvement created a truly innovative atmosphere that encouraged experimentation. I look forward to seeing Kodak Moments bring this experience to the market.
